The model parameters are as follows, take a ship part of the deck as 
an example, the plate frame structure length L =40 m, width B =8 m. 
deck thickness D =25 mm. deck under the T profile and longitudinal 
reinforcement. Define the material density of the plate frame as 7850 
kg/m3, the modulus of elasticity as E =2.06 ×1011 N/m2, Poisson’s 
ratio as 0.3, and the finite element mesh is divided into 14,240 cells with 
the cell type selected as shell181. The finite element model of the deck 
and its constraint situation with the vehicle moving route are shown in 
Fig. 8:
To better simulate the deck conditions, rigid fixed constraints are 
used around the plate frame. To facilitate the subsequent selection of 
response node coordinates and loading of the load, the plate frame 
model was shifted until the coordinates of the starting position of the 
shifted load were (0, 0, 0). The traveling route is shown as the red line 
segment in Fig. 8(d), the arrow is the end position, and there are 200 
units and 201 nodes on the traveling road. In this case, double time- 
varying load are used to simulate the axle weights of the front and 
rear vehicles, where the ratio of the front and rear axle weights is taken 
to be 1:1, and the axle spacing between the front and rear vehicles is 8 m.
The location of the measurement points is selected as shown in Fig. 9, 
the longitudinal interval of each measurement point being 1/8 of the 
deck length, and the transverse with the deck edge being 2 m. The co
ordinates of the measurement points are N1 (5, 2, 0), N2 (10, -2, 0), N3 
(15, 2, 0), N4 (20, -2, 0), N5 (25, 2, 0), N6 (30, -2, 0), and N7 (35, 2, 0).
The deck damping is defined using Rayleigh damping, where the 
coefficients α, and β are calculated as follows: 
α=2ω1ω2ξ/(ω1+ω2)
β= 2ξ/(ω1+ω2)
where ξ denotes the damping coefficient of steel, between 0.003 and 0.005, in this paper we take 0.005; ω1 denotes the first natural frequency 
of this plate structure; ω2 denotes the second natural frequency of the 
plate structure; ω1 and ω2 are calculated by ANSYS modal analysis.
The computational methodology uses the transient dynamics module 
in ANSYS to bring the time-varying load into the finite element model 
for the solution. The displacement and acceleration responses under 
seven measurement points were extracted for each condition and input 
into the AMF-BiGRU model for multi-modal fusion, and the output 
features were the front and rear axle weights and front and rear wheel 
positions. For the moving load reconstruction and localization results, 
the results when the front and rear wheels are not on and off the deck are 
0. For the localization results, when the vehicle is on the deck, the output 
is the distance of the load from the left end of the deck.
